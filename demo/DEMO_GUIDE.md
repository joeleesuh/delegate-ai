## Video Recording Tips

### Technical Setup
- **Resolution**: Record in 1080p or 4K
- **Browser**: Use Chrome/Firefox in full-screen (F11)
- **Screen recording software**:
  - OBS Studio (free)
  - Loom (easy, browser-based)
  - ScreenFlow (Mac)
  - Camtasia (Windows/Mac)
- **Audio**: Use good microphone, quiet room
- **Cursor**: Make sure cursor is visible in recording

### Visual Tips
- **Pace**: Move slowly through the interface - let viewers see details
- **Highlights**: Use your cursor to point to specific numbers and text
- **Zoom**: Consider zooming browser (Ctrl/Cmd +) to 110-125% for better visibility
- **Pauses**: Pause briefly when transitioning between sections
- **Smooth scrolling**: Scroll slowly so text is readable

### Presentation Tips
- **Energy**: Speak with enthusiasm - this is exciting!
- **Clarity**: Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- **Personality**: Be yourself - you're an MIT student solving a real problem
- **Authenticity**: You actually ARE the target user, emphasize that
- **Confidence**: You know this space - show it

### Recording Workflow
1. **Practice run**: Do the demo 2-3 times before recording
2. **Script familiarity**: Know the talking points but don't read robotically
3. **Multiple takes**: Record 2-3 full versions, pick the best
4. **Sections**: Or record in sections and edit together
5. **Editing**: Cut out any mistakes, add transitions if needed

## Questions You Might Get (FAQ)

**Q: Is this really AI or just transcription?**
A: It's real AI analysis. We use Claude API to analyze transcripts, identify themes, extract sentiment, recognize patterns across meetings, and generate actionable recommendations. It's doing reasoning, not just transcribing.

**Q: Do people object to an AI attending their meetings?**
A: The AI always announces itself at the start: "Hi, I'm DelegateAI representing Joel. I'm here to listen and take notes." Transparency is key. We find people appreciate that their rep is making an effort to engage even when they can't attend physically.

**Q: What about privacy and recording consent?**
A: Zoom bots must announce themselves and recording must be consented to. We're building with privacy-first approach - encrypted storage, clear data policies, and compliance with university/organizational rules.

**Q: How is this different from just recording Zoom meetings?**
A: Three ways: (1) AI analyzes and summarizes - saves hours of review time, (2) AI finds patterns ACROSS meetings - sees systemic issues, (3) AI generates specific action recommendations - tells you what to do about it.

**Q: What's your business model?**
A: SaaS subscriptions. Individual reps $29/month, teams $99/month, institutions $299/month. Low enough for student gov budgets, high enough to be sustainable.

**Q: Why will this succeed?**
A: (1) I'm the customer - founder/market fit, (2) Real problem - every rep struggles with this, (3) No competition - no AI solution exists, (4) Large TAM - representative roles everywhere, (5) Perfect timing - AI + remote work trends align.
